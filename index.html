About

Hi there! I am currently a senior (final year) undergraduate student majoring in data science at the University of Illinois Chicago. I am passionate about addressing complexities and challenges in sustainability and conservation through developing machine learning applications. In my time as an undergraduate student, I have had the incredible opportunity of researching the viability of using social media image data for large-scale wildlife population monitoring under the guidance of Dr. Tanya Berger-Wolf. In doing so, I've been able to merge my admiration for our biospheres and the countless forms of life withing them with my passion for unpacking and applying algorithms for data cleaning, feature extraction, data visualization, and machine learning.
My research has been my primary focus during my undergrad years, and has ultimately culminated in a profound sense of responsibility to take action and harness the power of data through developing machine learning applications to make sense of it all. As I move forward from my undergraduate studies, I plan on pursuing a PhD in computer science that would ultimately grant me the opportunity for not just lifelong learning, but further contribution to the research community with a special focus in sustainability and conservation applications - domains that are only beginning to receive some of the most recent advanced applications in the tech field.
As the role of technology transforms and transcends its role in our society, I believe a baseline technical literacy should be provided to any person that is affected by our ever present technologies. As a latina in a field that suffers from underrepresentation, I seek to contribute to my community and offer mentorship to students entering the tech field. Participating in outreach and mentorship programs is something that I hold with great importance as I continue on my journey to learn more about the field of ML/AI.
